Dual-Language Development Program

Our bilingual teachers conduct activities in both English and Spanish throughout the day, taking advantage of young children's natural language acquisition abilities. Frequently Asked Questions About Enrichment Classes

What are your hours of operation and what age groups do you accept?
We operate Monday through Friday from 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Our preschool program accepts children ages 2.5 to 5 years old, while our summer camp program serves children ages 3-6. We offer both full-time and part-time enrollment options, with a minimum of 2 days per week attendance required. All children must be potty trained before entering our 3+ programs, though we do accept children still working on potty training in our 2.5-year-old class.
What is your current monthly tuition rate and what does it include?
Monthly tuition for full-time enrollment (M-F, 7 AM-6 PM) is $1,495. Part-time options start at $725 for 2 days per week. Tuition includes all daily activities, enrichment classes (music, art, and movement), healthy snacks twice daily, and access to our secure parent communication app. We offer a 10% sibling discount and a 5% discount for paying quarterly. Registration fee is $150 annually, with summer camp having a separate $100 registration fee. Please note that lunch is not included and must be provided from home.
What curriculum do you follow and what makes your program unique?
We follow a play-based, developmentally appropriate curriculum that aligns with Washington State Early Learning Guidelines. Our program is unique because we incorporate daily nature-based learning activities in our adjacent Forest Park location, weather permitting. We maintain a low student-to-teacher ratio (1:7 for preschool, 1:5 for younger groups) and our teachers all hold degrees in Early Childhood Education or related fields. Our curriculum includes weekly themes, STEAM activities, language development, and social-emotional learning components. We're also one of the few preschools in Mill Creek offering both Mandarin and Spanish language exposure.
What is your illness policy and how do you handle COVID-19 protocols?
We follow strict health guidelines to maintain a safe environment. Children must be fever-free (without medication) for 24 hours before returning to school. For COVID-19, we follow current Snohomish County Health Department guidelines, which include required isolation periods for positive cases and exposure protocols. We maintain enhanced cleaning procedures, regular hand washing schedules, and air purifiers in each classroom. If your child shows symptoms during the day, we'll contact you for immediate pickup. We provide daily health screenings and maintain transparent communication with families about any health concerns.
What does your summer camp program include and when can we register?
Our summer camp runs from mid-June through late August, with weekly themed sessions. Each week includes water play, outdoor adventures in Forest Park, art projects, science experiments, and special guests. Camp hours are 9 AM-4 PM with extended care available (7 AM-6 PM) for an additional fee. Weekly rates are $375 for full-day camp, with extended care costing an additional $85 per week. Summer camp registration opens February 1st for current families and February 15th for new families. Spots fill quickly, especially for our popular themes like 'Forest Explorers' and 'Junior Scientists'. A non-refundable deposit of $100 per week is required to hold your spot.
