Frequently Asked Questions About Full-Day Preschool Program

What are your hours of operation and do you offer flexible drop-off times?
Our preschool program runs Monday through Friday from 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM. We offer flexible drop-off between 7:00-9:00 AM to accommodate working parents' schedules. Our core educational programming begins at 9:00 AM, so we encourage arrival before then if possible. Late pick-up is available until 6:00 PM with advance notice, though additional fees apply after 6:00 PM.
What are your current tuition rates for full-time preschool enrollment?
Full-time tuition (M-F, 7:00 AM-6:00 PM) is $1,450 per month for preschoolers ages 3-5. This includes all daily activities, educational materials, and two healthy snacks. Lunch is not included but can be added for an additional $85/month. We offer a 10% sibling discount and require a $250 annual registration fee. Part-time options (2-3 days/week) are also available starting at $875/month.
What credentials and licensing does your facility maintain?
We are fully licensed by the Washington State Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF) and maintain all required state certifications. Our facility undergoes annual inspections and our staff are all certified in First Aid/CPR. All lead teachers hold degrees in Early Childhood Education or related fields, and we maintain a teacher-to-student ratio of 1:10 or better, exceeding state requirements.
What does your summer camp program include and when can I register?
Our summer camp program runs June through August for children ages 3-6. Weekly themes include nature exploration, art, science, and water play activities. The program includes outdoor activities in our secured playground and weekly special events. Summer camp registration opens February 1st for current families and February 15th for new families. Weekly rates are $375, with a minimum 2-week enrollment required. Space is limited to 40 children per week.
How do you handle food allergies and medical conditions?
We take allergies and medical conditions very seriously. Upon enrollment, parents complete a detailed health form identifying any allergies or medical needs. We maintain a nut-free facility and can accommodate most dietary restrictions. Our staff are trained in allergy management and emergency response. For children with specific medical conditions, we create individual care plans in consultation with parents and medical providers. All medications must be properly labeled and accompanied by a doctor's authorization form.
