Frequently Asked Questions About Parent-Child Playgroups

What age groups do you accept at The Preschool at Forest Park?
We welcome children ages 2.5 to 5 years old in our preschool program, and ages 3-7 for our summer camp sessions. Our parent-child playgroups are specifically designed for children ages 12 months to 2.5 years, where parents/caregivers participate alongside their little ones. We maintain small class sizes with a 1:6 teacher-to-student ratio to ensure personalized attention.
What are your current tuition rates and schedule options?
For the 2023-24 school year, our preschool tuition is $385/month for two days per week (T/Th), $485/month for three days (M/W/F), and $695/month for five days. Parent-child playgroups are $225 for an 8-week session, meeting once per week. Summer camp runs in two-week sessions at $450 per session. We offer a 10% sibling discount and a 5% discount for paying the full year in advance. Hours are 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM for preschool, with extended care available until 3:00 PM for an additional fee.
How do you handle food allergies and dietary restrictions?
We take food allergies very seriously at Forest Park. We are a nut-free facility, and our staff is trained in allergy management and EpiPen administration. Parents provide their child's snacks and lunch, and we maintain strict policies about not sharing food. We will work with you to create an individual care plan for your child's specific allergies and ensure all teachers and staff are informed. We also post allergy alerts in classrooms when necessary.
What's your approach to early childhood education?
We follow a play-based, developmentally appropriate curriculum that emphasizes social-emotional learning, creative expression, and kindergarten readiness skills. Our program incorporates elements of both Reggio Emilia and traditional preschool approaches. Daily activities include circle time, art, outdoor play in our nature-based playground, music, and structured learning activities. Being located in Edmonds, we also take advantage of our proximity to local parks and the Edmonds Beach for nature exploration activities.
What are your licensing credentials and staff qualifications?
We are fully licensed by the Washington State Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF) and maintain all required state certifications. Our facility undergoes annual inspections and safety reviews. All teachers have degrees in Early Childhood Education or related fields, with a minimum of 3 years teaching experience. Staff members are CPR/First Aid certified and complete 20 hours of continuing education annually. Background checks are required for all employees, and we maintain strict safety and security protocols.
